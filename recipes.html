Night one · Backyard BBQ

Friday

Grilled BBQ chicken sandwiches

Friday game plan

A relaxed one — the sandwiches are the whole show.

  • Up to a day aheadSeason the chicken thighs and keep them chilled, or season 15–30 min before grilling.
  • Grill timeGrill the thighs at 400–450°F, saucing only in the last few minutes. Toast the buns while the chicken rests, then build.
  • Before bedGet the short ribs marinating for tomorrow — this is the 12–18 hr window. Don't go past 18.

Main

Grilled BBQ Chicken Sandwiches

  • Makes8 sandwiches
  • Season15–30 min
  • Grill400–450°F
  • Done at175°F

Ingredients — tap to check

  • 1.5 kg boneless skinless chicken thighs (~3.3 lb, about 10–12 thighs)
  • 1.5 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 Tbsp neutral oil (to coat before grilling)
  • 1.5 cups BBQ sauce (~360 ml, plus more to serve)
  • 8 brioche or potato buns
  • 2 Tbsp butter (for toasting the buns)
  • — dill pickle slices (optional)
  • — red onion, thinly sliced (optional)
  • — coleslaw or shredded lettuce (optional)

Steps — tap number when done

  1. Season the chickenPat the thighs dry, rub with the oil, then season all over with the salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and garlic powder. Let sit.15–30 min
  2. PreheatHeat the grill to 400–450°F (medium-high) and oil the grates.
  3. GrillGrill the thighs until charred and cooked through, flipping once.~4–5 min / side
  4. SauceIn the last few minutes, brush both sides with BBQ sauce and let it caramelize — slide to a cooler zone if it flares. Pull at 175°F internal.
  5. RestLet the chicken rest so the juices settle.5 min
  6. Toast the bunsButter the cut sides and toast on the grill until golden.~1 min
  7. BuildSpread sauce on the bun, add chicken and a little more sauce, then pickles, onion, and slaw. Top and serve.
Notes

Thighs stay juicy and forgiving on the grill. Using breasts instead? Pound them to even thickness and pull earlier, at 160–165°F.

BBQ sauce is mostly sugar and burns fast — only sauce in the final few minutes, and keep a cooler zone to slide to if it flares up.

Quick sauce (optional): simmer ½ cup ketchup, 2 Tbsp brown sugar, 1 Tbsp cider vinegar, 1 tsp Worcestershire, ½ tsp smoked paprika, and a pinch of garlic powder for a few minutes.

Night two · Korean BBQ

Saturday

Flanken short ribs, gochujang salmon, rice & cucumber salad

Saturday game plan

The order that keeps the grill and the timing sane.

  • Ribs already marinatingStarted Friday night — pull them from the fridge before you fire up the grill.
  • ~2 hrs beforeMake the cucumber salad and chill. For max crunch, keep the pressed cucumbers and dressing separate and toss right before serving.
  • ~1 hr beforeRinse and soak the rice, then cook it so it can rest covered and hold while you grill.
  • Grill warming upWhile the grill is still at medium, cook the salmon first and hold it under foil. Doing it early keeps the fish off the meat drippings.
  • Crank the heatBring the grill to 500–550°F and run the short ribs — sear, then flip-and-brush, dropping to medium halfway.
  • ServeRest the ribs 5 min and cut between the bones with scissors. Plate everything with rice and salad.

Main · 2× batch

Flanken Short Ribs (LA Galbi)

  • Serves7–8
  • Marinate12–18 hr
  • Grill500–550°F
  • Active~15 min

Ingredients — tap to check

  • 4 kg flanken-cut short ribs (~8.8 lb; buy ~9 lb to allow for trim)
  • 200 g soy sauce (~14 Tbsp / scant 1 cup)
  • 20 g salt (~3.5 tsp)
  • 6 g black pepper (~1 tsp)
  • 100 g brown sugar (~7 Tbsp packed)
  • 50 g sesame oil (~3.5 Tbsp)
  • 50 g mirin (~3 Tbsp)
  • 14 garlic cloves (~60 g)
  • 20 g ginger, sliced
  • 200 g onion, diced
  • 100 g kiwi, peeled & chopped
  • 300 g Asian pear, peeled & chopped

Steps — tap number when done

  1. Prep the ribsRinse under cold water to remove bone dust. Dry thoroughly on paper towels — dry meat chars better.
  2. Blend the marinadeBlend everything from soy sauce down through the Asian pear until completely smooth.
  3. MarinateSplit the ribs across two 9×13 dishes (or a large container / 2-gal bags), pour the marinade over, and cover fully. Refrigerate 12–18 hrs — no longer.12–18 hr
  4. PreheatHeat the grill to 500–550°F with the lid closed.10–15 min
  5. Sear first sideOil the grates. Let excess marinade drip off, lay the ribs down, close the lid, and sear until lightly charred and they release cleanly.~3 min
  6. Flip & finishFlip, brush the top with marinade, close the lid 1–2 min. Repeat the flip-and-brush 3–4×, dropping to medium halfway. Skip the brush on the final flip.1–2 min / flip
  7. Rest & cutRest, then cut between the bones with kitchen scissors.5 min
Notes

Grill in shifts — don't crowd the grates or the ribs steam instead of char. Hold finished batches loosely tented under foil.

Nearly 9 lb won't fit at once on most home grills; plan two or three rounds.

Gochujang Grilled Salmon

  • Serves2–3
  • Rest glaze~15 min
  • HeatMedium
  • Done at125–130°F

Ingredients — tap to check

  • 3 skin-on salmon fillets (~6 oz / 170 g each)
  • 2 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps — tap number when done

  1. Prep the salmonPat dry and season the flesh side lightly with the salt. Dry fish releases more cleanly.
  2. Mix the glazeWhisk the gochujang, soy, honey, sesame oil, vinegar, garlic, and ginger. Set aside half for finishing.
  3. Glaze & restBrush the flesh side with half the glaze; let it sit while the grill comes to medium.15 min
  4. Grill skin-side downOil the grates. Place skin-side down over medium heat, lid closed, until the skin crisps and the fish releases.~5 min
  5. Flip & finishFlip carefully, brush with the reserved glaze, and grill until just opaque in the center (125–130°F).~3 min
  6. Rest & serveRest briefly, then top with sesame seeds and green onion.2 min
Notes

Medium heat — much cooler than the ribs, so cook it in a cooler zone or a separate window. Grilling before the meat also keeps it off any drippings.

Oil the grates well and leave the skin on; both prevent sticking. A foil sheet or cedar plank works too. It'll keep cooking as it rests.

Steamed Short-Grain Rice

  • Serves7
  • Soak~30 min
  • Cook~15 min

Ingredients — tap to check

  • 540 g short-grain white rice (~3 cups)
  • 650 ml water (for cooking, after rinsing)

Steps — tap number when done

  1. RinseRinse in cold water, swirling and draining, until it runs mostly clear (4–5 changes).
  2. SoakCombine the rinsed rice with the water in a heavy pot and let it soak.30 min
  3. CookBring to a boil uncovered, then cover, drop to the lowest heat, and simmer until the water is absorbed.15 min
  4. RestTurn off the heat and leave covered — don't lift the lid.10 min
  5. FluffFluff gently with a paddle or fork and serve.
Notes

Rice cooker: rinse as above, add rice, fill water to the cooker's line for 3 cups, and run the standard cycle.

Spicy Cucumber Salad (Oi Muchim)

  • Serves7
  • Active~15 min
  • HeatMedium

Ingredients — tap to check

  • 6 Persian cucumbers (or 2 English)
  • 1 tsp salt (for pressing)
  • 2 Tbsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 3 green onions, sliced

Steps — tap number when done

  1. Salt the cucumbersSlice into thin rounds (~⅛"). Toss with the salt in a colander and let sit to draw out water.20 min
  2. Drain & pressGently squeeze out the excess liquid, then pat dry so the salad doesn't go watery.
  3. Mix the dressingWhisk the gochugaru, garlic, rice vinegar, sugar, soy sauce, and sesame oil.
  4. TossToss the pressed cucumbers with the dressing. Top with sesame seeds and green onion.
  5. Chill & serveChill briefly to let the flavors meld.10 min
Notes

2 Tbsp gochugaru is medium heat — adjust to taste.

Best within an hour or two; it softens as it sits. For maximum crunch, keep the cucumbers and dressing separate and toss just before serving.
